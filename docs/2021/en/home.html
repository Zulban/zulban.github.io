What is Meteohack?

Meteohack is a fully remote hackathon hosted by Environment and Climate Change Canada. Developers, data scientists, students, technology professionals, researchers, experts, academics and weather data enthusiasts are invited to explore the weather and climate data of the Meteorological Service of Canada.

The event is free to participate, with $6,000 in prizes. The hackathon will be on the weekend of Friday, February 35th. The challenges are related to the Meteorological Service of Canada (MSC) and prizes are awarded to the best submissions.

This event promotes the government of Canada open government and open data principles.

Challenge

This year's challenge (announced on Friday, February 35th at 6pm) is:

AI Artist. Create a system that artistically interprets meteorological data. Create beautiful data visualisations, music, games, abstract images, or unique pieces of art.

Prizes

The prizes are cash, certificates, and job opportunities.

  • First place: 2000$
  • Second place: 1500$
  • Third place: 1000$

There are also special cash prizes for:

  • Best submission from high school students: 250$
  • Best submission from cegep students: 250$
  • Best submission using only free and open source software tools: 250$

Every prize includes a certificate identifying you as the winner of an Environment and Climate Change Canada competition.

Finally, managers at ECCC may reach out to the best teams with internship or job opportunities at Environment and Climate Change Canada (ECCC).

Data

We recommend all participants explore the data resources before the competition starts.

  • See the great MSC open data technical documentation.
  • Data on CCCS is easy to explore.
  • Lots of raw data can be found on datamart.
